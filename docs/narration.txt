Archon is a unified financial intelligence platform. It brings a business's whole financial picture — invoices, payments, bank statements, payroll and expenses — into one place, and gives an agent a memory that persists and can be queried across completely separate sessions. Everything you are about to see runs live, right now, on Alibaba Cloud, powered by real Qwen models.

First, the proof it is real. A single call to the health endpoint on our Alibaba box shows the embedder is Qwen text-embedding-v4 and the narrator is qwen-plus, producing genuine one-thousand-and-twenty-four dimension embeddings. No stubs, no fakes.

Now the heart of the MemoryAgent track: cross-session persistence. In Session A, the agent has reconciled a bank confirmation, a payroll register and the individual payslips into one accurate event, and it commits the salient facts to memory: the true cost of employing the team, and the gap the bank statement alone never shows. The memories are embedded with text-embedding-v4 and written to pgvector on Alibaba Cloud. Then Session A ends. The client disconnects. Nothing is kept in process memory; the facts now live only in the database.

Session B is a completely fresh, later session. A different client, no shared variables, no cache; it has only a question. What did it truly cost to employ the team last month, compared with what actually left the bank? Qwen embeds the question, runs a cosine nearest-neighbour search over pgvector, and qwen-plus writes a grounded, cited answer, recalling that the true cost of employing the team was fifteen thousand eight hundred euros against just ten thousand that left the bank: a five thousand eight hundred euro gap. The scores are real cosine similarities over the vector index. A memory written in one session was recalled, by meaning, in a separate one. That is cross-session persistence.

And the memory is genuinely queryable, not a canned reply. A second, different question, the total cost of employing one named person, returns a fresh grounded answer from the same persistent memory.

Finally, the deployment. This runs on Alibaba Cloud: an ECS instance in Singapore, pgvector with an HNSW cosine index holding the memory, and Qwen text-embedding-v4 and qwen-plus from Model Studio. Persistent, queryable, and across sessions. The MemoryAgent track, proven live.
